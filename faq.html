FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the main purpose of a fan?

Fans are used for circulating air in rooms and buildings; for cooling motors and transmissions; for cooling and drying people, materials, or products; for exhausting dust and noxious fumes; for conveying light materials; for forced draft in steam boilers; and in heating, ventilating, and air-conditioning systems.

What causes a fan to stop working?

This could be because the circuit breaker has tripped or is off. Check your panel to confirm this. If the circuit breaker works fine, it could be a problem with loose wiring and connections. Your ceiling fan could also stop working due to internal defects namely shot ball bearings or an overheated motor.

What to do if fan is not rotating?

possible Issues

  • Check the Cord.
  • Check Your Circuit Breaker.
  • Check for Power to Your Outlet.
  • Check the Fan Fuse.
  • Replace the Cord.
  • Clean the Fan.
  • Grease the Motor.
  • Call Customer Support.

Why do fans rotate slowly?

Once the capacitor stops working, power cannot be transformed to the motor. Further, this can cause your ceiling fan to run slower than the usual speed.

My fan makes noise while working

A ceiling fan making a grinding noise may be caused by unbalanced blades that make the fan wobble. Ceiling fan blades can become unbalanced by dust collecting on the blades when the fan isn't in use. Clean the blades by wiping them down with a damp rag to remove any dust and debris causing them to be unbalanced.
